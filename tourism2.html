Discover Oman ... like never before

Wadi Bani Khalid

Ash Sharqiah North Governorate

The Northern part of the greater Ash Sharqiah Governorate is home to Al Qabil, Al Mudhaibi, and Wadi Bani Khalid among other attractive destinations. Blending the agricultural environment made possible by the coastal climate with the nomadic way of life has rendered the locals of Ash Sharqiah as farmers who are still weary of the desert and keen to its secrets.

Bat

Ad Dhahirah Governorate

Ad Dhahirah is Oman's premier oil producing governorate, totalling around a third of all oil exports from the Sultanate. The region holds a very rich and ancient history ionised with the Bat region, a UNESCO World Heritage site with tombs dating back to 4000 BC. Geographically the governorate sits between the urban centres of the Eastern Arabian Peninsula and has always been an important trade centre for the region.

Nizwa

Ad Dakhiliyah Governorate

Linking the coast to the interior of Oman, Ad Dakhiliyah sits on the slopes of the western Hajar Mountains. In the early days of Islam, Nizwa formed the capital of the Sultanate and played a big role in the spread of the faith in the country. Dubbed "the egg of Islam", Nizwa was home to the Ibadhi sect of Islam. The entire governorate sits some 3000 metres above sea level and is home to lush mountainous terrain and holds significant agricultural importance growing dates, pomegranates, and nuts.

Rub' Al Khali (The Empty Quarter)

Al Wusta Governorate

Occupying a large central portion of the Sultanate with the Arabian Sea to the East and the Empty Quarter to the West, Al Wusta or 'the central' Governorate is home to a plethora of wildlife including more than 130 species of birds and rare mamals such as the Arabian oryx and the Nubian ibex. The governorate can in some ways be considered a huge natural reserve, as other than the main highway linking Muscat to Salalah, there are no tarmac roads interfering with the wildlife and the natural landscape.
