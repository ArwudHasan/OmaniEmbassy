Discover Oman ... like never before

Khasab

Musandam Governorate

Adjacent to the Strait of Hormuz, one of the world's most important trading routes, Khasab is a town made distinct by various ruins and unique architecture. Bait Al-Qufl, or 'House of the Lock', (see image) is a series of fortified buildings that were designed for the storage of goods and grains centuries ago. Some of these structures are still in use today.

Salalah

Dhofar Governorate

Known throughout the Sultanate as the southern capital of Oman, the culturally vibrant city with its bustling market places is historically reminiscent of the once-colony of Zanzibar in eastern Africa. A temperate climate and tens of kilometres of white beaches makes Salalah a traveller favourite.

Muttrah Souk

Muscat Governorate

Within the naturally beautiful and historically rich capital city of Muscat, Muttrah Souk (marketplace) takes visitors on a culturally captivating journey of the senses. Stalls that carry a variety of goods, ranging from frankincense to Bedouin handcrafted items to traditional jewellery and spices brings the narrow pathways of the Souk to life. Muscat city is a great place to start exploring Oman.

Rustaq

Al Batinah Governorate

Once consider a capital of Oman, Rustaq is home to one of the oldest forts in Oman, Rustaq Fort, which is dated to the 3rd century CE. Rustaq also houses areas of therapeutic hot springs rich in sulphur, most notably Ain Al-Kasafa. The ancient town is also home to traditional arts like the Mashia and the Razhaa, the traditional dances where men use swords to dance to drum beats making for an enriching spectacle.
